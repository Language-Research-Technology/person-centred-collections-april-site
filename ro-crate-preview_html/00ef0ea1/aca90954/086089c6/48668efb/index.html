Map

This map was drawn to teach young Anmatyerr people about their countries, so that they can see where they are from and which families they are connected to. It was also drawn so that young people can look at their skin groups as well as those of their grandfathers and other families.

Nwern-kenh Mer “Our Country” April Pengart Campbell, 2005.

Teaching about Country and skin names in the Ti Tree School classroom

Our Country

Map nhenh nwern-kenh mer-akert. Map nhenhel ilem nwern-kenh ikwang map. Nwern family map anem mer nhenh mapek-artwey. Tyerrty Anmatyerr nyent-antey anem. Artekerr-artek mangkem mer arrpenh-warn alhem. Kwer-penh nwern-kenh anem merek-artwey, nyent-antey. Ingkerreny tyerrty arlalty nwern anem.

This map shows our country. It shows our skin groups. Our families are traditional owners for this country. The Anmatyerr people are all one. They branch out like roots into other country. Then our families are landowners, all one together. All of them are our family.

Colour map anem, ilem mer nwern-kenh. Nthenh-areny apek nwern anem? Colour map pwert anem, lwerenhan kwenh ilem, nwern family angerr anem. Arelh ampwang mapel artwang aywangel-thwen antwerrkem angkety angerr. Angwenhakey-akert, anengkerr-akert, alyelhenty, arlkeny, mpatyel ilem ahern-warn. Nwern anyenem nwern-kenh mer lterrk anthwerr.

The colours show our country. Which is our traditional country? All the colours are close together, that tells us we are all a big family. The old women and men have a big story. With lots of things, Dreaming stories, songs, ceremonial body markings, and sand drawings. We hold onto our country really strongly.

Nwern kwenh alherl-anem mer nwern-kenh-warn, nwern arntarnt-arelhetyek nwern-kenh merlalp map. Nwern ilelhetyek merek-artwey ineh, nwernarl petyek, mer aretyek. Nwern awelhetyek irrernt irlkang Angkety alhem. Yanhan ilem merlalp map angkem anantherreh mwerr. Anantherr kangkwerrenty-kwenh anem, anantherr kwenh irlkert-irrem, mer apek anety-ilerleng.

When we go to visit our country, we need to watch out for ourselves from the ancestral spirits. We need to announce our arrival to those landowners, that we have come to look at the country. We will feel the welcome of a cold breeze. That tells us that the spirits are happy with us. If we don’t obey (the law) we will get sick, not respecting our country.

Mer nwern-kenh-angkwerr ngkwey anthwerr mwang kwelay alhetyam, mpwarenhetyam kwaty map arnerr-arlk irntang kernel. Kwer-penhan yang kwet-anem pety-alpek mer Rrkwer-warn. Nwern anyenem mer yanh pwert-antey. Nwern kwenh arntarnt-arelhetyek nwern-kenh merlalp map. Kwer-penh nwern kwer map kalty-anthetyek, inangemek antwerrketyenheng angkety nhenh. Inang awelhetyenheng mwerr anthwerr.

Long ago the rainbow snake went through our country and made the water holes and rockholes on top of the hills. Then he returned to Brooke’s Soak. We keep the country all together. We need to respect our ancestral spirits. Then we should teach the children, so they can grab hold of this story. And listen and learn really carefully, so they can feel good about themselves.
